2.AUTOMATED STREET LIGHT CONTROLLING SYSTEM

This project encompasses an automatic street light control system designed to operate during nighttime hours, optimizing energy usage and providing adequate lighting in urban environments after sunset.An automatic street light control system optimizes energy usage and enhances nighttime visibility in urban areas. Using light sensors, like photoresistors or photodiodes, the system activates street lights at sunset when ambient light levels drop below a certain threshold, and deactivates them at sunrise when light levels rise. Motion sensors further enhance efficiency by adjusting brightness levels or switching off lights in inactive areas. A central control unit coordinates the system, employing scheduling algorithms to adapt to factors such as traffic patterns. Energy-efficient LED bulbs are crucial for minimizing power consumption, with fixtures designed for optimal light distribution and reduced glare. Power can be sourced from the grid or renewable options like solar panels, offering sustainability benefits. Overall, this system offers a practical and sustainable solution, reducing costs, minimizing light pollution, and improving safety in urban environments.
